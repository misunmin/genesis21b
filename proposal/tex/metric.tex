\textbf{Decision Gate Metrics and Go/No-Go Criteria}

Phase~I success will be evaluated through quantitative metrics that
demonstrate predictive accuracy, computational efficiency, scalability,
and measurable AI advantage relative to conventional high-fidelity
simulation workflows. The following metrics define the Phase~I
go/no-go decision criteria.

\textbf{1. Data Integration and Latent Representation Fidelity}

A unified, geometry-aware dataset integrating heterogeneous
Nek5000/RS simulation data and experimental datasets will be
constructed. Successful completion will be demonstrated if:

\begin{itemize}
\setlength{\itemsep}{3pt}

\item $>95\%$ of selected simulation and experimental datasets are
successfully mapped into a common latent representation.

\item Reconstruction error of encoded flow fields remains below
$3\%$ relative $L_2$ norm error for representative thermal–fluid fields.

\item Latent representations demonstrate consistent embedding
of multiple geometry classes (wire bundles, ICF configurations,
and coil inserts) within a shared latent space.

\end{itemize}

\textbf{2. Predictive Accuracy Across Unseen Configurations}

The geometry-aware foundation model will be evaluated for
generalization capability across previously unseen geometries.
Successful completion requires:

\begin{itemize}
\setlength{\itemsep}{3pt}

\item Prediction accuracy better than $5\%$ relative error for
key engineering quantities, including pressure drop and
heat-transfer coefficients.

\item Stable prediction performance across at least
three distinct geometry classes and multiple Reynolds-number regimes.

\end{itemize}

\textbf{3. Closed-Loop Optimization Efficiency}

Latent-space optimization workflows will be evaluated relative
to brute-force CFD-based design studies. Phase~I success
requires:

\begin{itemize}
\setlength{\itemsep}{3pt}

\item Demonstration of closed-loop optimization achieving
at least $5$--$10\times$ reduction in required high-fidelity
simulation evaluations relative to baseline parameter sweeps.

\item Successful convergence of multi-objective optimization
problems balancing heat-transfer enhancement and pressure-drop
penalty.

\end{itemize}

\textbf{4. Demonstration of AI Advantage Through Scaling Behavior}

AI advantage will be quantified through scaling metrics that
demonstrate improved performance with increasing data and
computational resources. Successful completion requires:

\begin{itemize}
\setlength{\itemsep}{3pt}

\item Demonstration of improved prediction accuracy with
increasing training dataset size (e.g., error reduction of
$\geq 30\%$ when training data volume is increased by $2\times$).

\item Demonstration of efficient scaling on GPU-enabled
HPC platforms, showing reduction in training or inference
time proportional to available compute resources.

\end{itemize}

\textbf{Go/No-Go Criterion}

Progression to Phase~II will require demonstration that
the integrated foundation-model and optimization framework
achieves all of the following:

\begin{itemize}
\setlength{\itemsep}{3pt}

\item Prediction accuracy $\leq 5\%$ for engineering-relevant
quantities across unseen geometries,

\item Reduction of required CFD evaluations by
at least $5\times$ relative to brute-force workflows,

\item Demonstration of measurable scaling-based AI advantage
with increasing training data and computational resources.

\item Demonstration of end-to-end design-cycle
time reduction of at least $10\times$
relative to traditional simulation-driven workflows.

\end{itemize}

Meeting these criteria will validate the feasibility of
foundation-model–driven, agentic AI workflows for
accelerated thermal–fluid design and justify advancement
to Phase~II development.
