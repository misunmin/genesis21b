\noindent
Phase~I success will be evaluated using quantitative metrics that assess
whether the proposed foundation-model and agentic-AI workflow provides a clear
AI advantage over conventional CFD-only design. The decision gates focus on
four areas: 

\noindent
{\bf 1. Latent representation fidelity.} 
Demonstrate that the heterogeneous datasets can be mapped into a common
geometry-aware latent space with relative reconstruction errors below 5\% for
the primary thermal-fluid state variables and below 3\% for derived QOIs such
as pressure drop and heat-transfer.

\noindent
{\bf 2. Predictive accuracy on unseen cases.} 
Demonstrate mean prediction errors below 5\% for QOIs on held-out geometries
and operating conditions across at least three configurations.

\noindent
{\bf 3. Reduction in required CFD evaluations.} 
Demonstrate that the latent-space optimization identifies designs of comparable
or better quality than conventional parameter sweep workflows while using at
least 5$\times$ fewer high-fidelity CFD evaluations, with 10$\times$ as the
target value.

\noindent
{\bf 4. End-to-end design acceleration.} 
Demonstrate at least 5$\times$ acceleration in design-cycle time for
representative fluid-thermal device.  Additional evidence provided if increased
training data systematically improves prediction accuracy or optimization
efficiency.
\\[-2ex]


\noindent
{\bf Advancement to Phase II} will require demonstration of: 
(i) prediction errors $ <  \, 5$\% on unseen cases and
(ii)  $\approx \, 5\times$ reduction in the required number of high-fidelity 
      CFD evaluations.
