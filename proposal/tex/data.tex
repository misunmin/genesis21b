The Argonne team has extensive experience in LES of turbulent flow in liquid
metal fast reactors, spanning configurations from isolated subassemblies
\cite{fischer08a,Dai2025WireWrapped61Pin,shaver37pin,shavernacie} to 
full-cores reactor geometries~\cite{sc22,gb23}.  
   A substantial portion of these datasets have been generated and
is available for immediate use. These datasets are well documented in 
in the nuclear design literature and provide validated benchmarks for 
thermal–fluid performance prediction.  
  In particular we have access to {\em experimentally validated}
datasets for a 61-pin assembly over a range of Reynolds numbers
\cite{Dai2025WireWrapped61Pin}, as well as satasets for a 19-pin assembly under
multiple flow and heating conditions~\cite{shavernacie}.  
  Similarly, the MFEM team possesses high-resolution simulation datasets
from model ICF studies spanning wide range of operating
regimes, geometries and spatial resolutions.  
{\bf Both Nek5000/RS and MFEM have infrastructure to automatically produce additional
data, some of which is being generated on  OLCF Frontier, ALCF Aurora, and the
Argonne NSE Divison cluster, {\em Nek5k}, as part of ongoing studies.}
These efforts include simulations of
7-pin reflector assemblies and additional 37-pin configurations similar to those
previsouly studied by Shaver~\cite{shaver37pin}.  The most recent and highly detailed
experimental datasets for wire-wrapped bundles are available in~\cite{kaeri37pin},
and will be incorporated into model training and validation.

A similar data-generation framework exists for wire-coil insert geometries, 
which provide a computationally efficient platform for workflow testing and 
method development.  
% The data situation is similar for the wire-coil insert geometry, which is a
% low-cost case to be used for test/development of the overall workflow.
Extensive high-quality experimental datasets are available in \cite{collins22}
and have been corroborated through Nek5000/RS simulations by Kaneko \cite{kaneko22}, 
inclduing studies of related configurations 
%%%%who also examined nearby geometric configurations} 
such as arrays of ring inserts.
These cases are computationally inexpensive and can typically be executed on
one or two GPUs within approximately one hour 
to generate requisite QOIs, such as the Nusselt number (Nu), 
a nondimensional heat-transfer coefficient.  
{\color{red}CHECK:MMS Importantly, the Nusselt number in these configurations exhibits strong 
sensitivity to geometric variation, with increases of up to fourfold observed 
in both experiments and simulations for optimized geometries.
This strong geometry–performance coupling provides an ideal testbed for 
evaluating automated AI-driven optimization workflows and demonstrating 
measurable performance gains.
